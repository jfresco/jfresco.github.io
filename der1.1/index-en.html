Projects

  • DemocracyOS is an open-source web application designed to improve the chances of citizen to participate in decision-making processes. This is the technological manifestation of our main mision: to bring the political system closer to those behaviors and technologies of this century.

    Net Party

  • Civic stack is a showcase for open-source civic tech. It has been designed collaboratively as a window to show together all these technologies that are meant to empower citizens.

    civicstack.org

  • Demos Legislatura Porteña is a project we did in collaboration with the Legislature of the City of Buenos Aires. The experience allowed every citizen of Buenos Aires to get informed, debate and vote on 3 pieces of proposed legislation selected by themselves. An institutional bet on the idea of collective intelligence.

    demos.legislatura.gov.ar

  • Demos Bahia Blanca is an initiative in collaboration with the Agency of Open Gov from the city of Bahia Blanca. We face the challenge of using technology to open legislation to citizen debate.

    bahiablanca.democracyos.org

  • SOLE crafts self-organized learning environments, based on the pedagogy of Sugata Mitra. It involves many children, a few computers and one big question. The Internet transforms the way we learn, also in the schools of Buenos Aires.

    soleargentina.org

  • Academia is a space where artists, researchers and those who have been experimenting and thinking the intersection between technology and power come together. Democracy in the Internet Era brings new questions and surprising scenarios. We explore them.

  • UPDATE project was born in Sao Paulo, with a group of activists who started to ask if it was possible to build a new category in social activism: the Political Hacker. We collaborate mapping and connecting activists who use the Internet to question and re-shape the way of making politics inside and outside the traditional institutions.

    demos.legislatura.gov.ar

  • Tres Por Tres (3 ON 3) is a series of conversations held by many people on specific subjects like electronic voting, candidate’s promises of openness and digital divide. Three organizations host three events to discuss, without speakers or lecterns, the impact of technology in Argentine politics.

Who we are

We come together from many different places with something in common: we all use the Internet, we believe in politics and we see a democratic system that needs to upgrade. The more voices can express and be heard, the better democracy we will have. We are artists, researchers, developers, designers, activists, entrepreneurs. We are human. We like to think systems.
